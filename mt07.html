Full Description

The Yamaha MT-07 has earned a reputation as one of the most versatile and approachable motorcycles in the middleweight naked bike segment. First introduced in 2014, the MT-07 has undergone continual refinement to deliver a balance of performance, agility, and affordability. Its 2024 iteration builds on this legacy, offering a modernized design, improved components, and a user-friendly ride, appealing to both new riders and seasoned enthusiasts alike. The MT-07 exemplifies Yamaha's "Hyper Naked" philosophy, with an aggressive design and a focus on raw, exhilarating performance.


Engine and Performance: At the heart of the MT-07 is Yamaha’s renowned 689cc CP2 parallel-twin engine, featuring a crossplane crankshaft design that delivers smooth, linear power and exceptional torque. Producing approximately 73.8 horsepower and 67 Nm of torque, the engine excels in mid-range performance, making it ideal for city riding, spirited backroad runs, and even highway cruising. The throttle response is precise, and the six-speed transmission ensures smooth gear shifts with a well-matched final drive ratio. The bike's lightweight and punchy engine create a lively and engaging ride, especially for those who appreciate strong low and mid-range grunt.


Chassis and Handling: The MT-07’s lightweight diamond steel frame is key to its agile handling, offering a combination of rigidity and flexibility hat enhances rider confidence. The 2024 model features an upgraded suspension system, with a 41mm KYB front fork and a preload-adjustable rear mono-shock, tuned for a balance of comfort and sporty performance. Weighing in at just 406 lbs (184 kg) wet, the MT-07 feels nimble and easy to maneuver, making it an excellent choice for navigating tight urban streets or carving through twisty mountain roads. The wide handlebars and upright seating position further enhance its intuitive handling characteristics.


Braking and Safety: The MT-07 is equipped with dual 298mm discs at the front and a single 245mm disc at the rear, paired with advanced ABS as standard. This braking setup delivers strong, consistent stopping power with excellent feedback, even under hard braking. The inclusion of ABS provides an added layer of safety, especially in adverse conditions, preventing wheel lock-up and enhancing rider confidence. While the bike lacks more advanced electronic rider aids like traction control, its well-calibrated braking and manageable power delivery ensure a secure ride for most conditions.


Design and Features: The MT-07’s bold, aggressive styling is a hallmark of Yamaha’s Hyper Naked lineup, featuring sharp, angular lines, a muscular tank design, and a minimalist tail section. The 2024 model boasts full LED lighting, including a distinctive bifunctional projector headlight that enhances visibility and modern aesthetics. The bike’s compact dimensions and clean design elements emphasize its lightweight nature while maintaining a striking road presence. Its color options and finish quality are designed to appeal to a wide audience, from newcomers to experienced riders seeking a stylish and capable machine.


Ergonomics and Comfort: Ergonomically, the MT-07 is designed to accommodate a wide range of riders with its upright riding position, low 31.7-inch (805mm) seat height, and neutral footpeg placement. The seat is redesigned for improved comfort, allowing for extended riding sessions without excessive fatigue. The wide handlebars provide excellent leverage for steering, making the bike feel light and manageable even for less experienced riders. With its balanced seating position, the MT-07 is versatile enough for city commutes, spirited rides, or long-distance touring.


Technology and Features: The MT-07 is equipped with a full-color TFT display, a significant upgrade from previous models, providing a modern interface that displays essential information such as speed, RPM, fuel level, and gear position. The system also supports smartphone connectivity, allowing riders to access notifications, calls, and more through the Yamaha MyRide app. While the MT-07 lacks advanced rider modes or adjustable traction control, it focuses on simplicity and reliability, ensuring that the rider remains fully engaged with the machine.


Conclusion: The Yamaha MT-07 continues to be a benchmark in the middleweight naked bike category, combining exhilarating performance, rider-friendly ergonomics, and modern technology in a lightweight package. Its approachable nature makes it a great choice for beginners, while its dynamic engine and sporty handling offer plenty of excitement for experienced riders. Whether you're looking for a commuter, a weekend canyon carver, or a versatile all-rounder, the MT-07 delivers a compelling blend of value, performance, and style that few bikes in its class can match.
