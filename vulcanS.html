Full Description

The Kawasaki Vulcan S is a unique cruiser that blends modern technology, versatility, and easy handling for a wide range of riders. Unlike traditional cruisers, the Vulcan S features a more approachable design with a sportier edge, appealing to those who want a cruiser with a lighter, more agile feel. Designed with a low seat height and a highly customizable riding position, it is well-suited for both new riders and those seeking a more relaxed, yet powerful, motorcycle. With its refined styling, impressive comfort, and all-around performance, the Vulcan S stands as one of the standout models in the mid-weight cruiser segment.


Engine and Performance: The Vulcan S is powered by a 649cc parallel-twin engine derived from the Ninja 650, producing around 61 horsepower and 64 Nm of torque. This engine delivers smooth, linear power that provides ample acceleration for cruising on highways and around town. The torque is well-balanced, offering plenty of low-end grunt for relaxed rides while still providing enough punch at higher revs to make highway cruising enjoyable. Coupled with a 6-speed gearbox, the engine’s responsive nature makes the Vulcan S a versatile cruiser, allowing for spirited riding when desired without sacrificing ease of control for beginner riders.


Chassis and Handling: The Vulcan S features a modern, tubular steel frame designed to provide stability while keeping the bike light and agile. With a low curb weight of approximately 492 lbs (223 kg), the Vulcan S feels nimble and easy to maneuver, even for riders who are not yet accustomed to larger cruisers. The front end is fitted with a 41mm telescopic fork, offering smooth ride quality over most road surfaces, while the rear mono-shock features a remote pre-load adjuster for easy suspension adjustments. This setup allows the bike to handle urban environments with ease while still providing enough stability for highway cruising and cornering.


Braking and Safety: The Vulcan S is equipped with a 300mm front disc brake and a 250mm rear disc brake, both with dual-piston calipers that offer solid stopping power. The bike’s braking system is responsive, allowing riders to easily modulate the brakes for smooth, controlled stops. While the Vulcan S does not feature advanced rider aids like ABS in the standard model, it does come with a well-tuned braking system that is both reliable and easy to use. For riders seeking additional safety, ABS is available as an option on the bike, further enhancing its stopping capability, particularly in adverse weather or emergency situations.


Design and Features: The Vulcan S has a minimalist design with clean lines, a low-slung profile, and modern elements that distinguish it from more traditional cruisers. The low-sitting fuel tank, wide handlebar, and ergonomic design create a bold, aggressive look, while the low seat height (27.8 inches) enhances its accessibility for shorter riders. The bike features bright LED lighting, including the front headlamp, tail light, and turn signals, which contribute to better visibility and a more contemporary aesthetic. The Vulcan S also includes a digital instrument panel that provides essential riding data such as speed, fuel level, and trip information in a clear, easy-to-read format.


Ergonomics and Comfort: The Vulcan S stands out in adjustable ergonomics, designed to accommodate a wide range of rider sizes and preferences. The bike features an adjustable "Ergo-Fit" system, which allows riders to customize the footpegs, seat, and handlebars to suit their specific body type, ensuring a comfortable and personalized fit. The seat is plush, providing good support for longer rides, and the low seat height makes it easy for riders to place their feet flat on the ground when stopped. Combined with the relaxed riding position, these ergonomic features make the Vulcan S a comfortable choice for both short and long rides.


Technology and Features: The Vulcan S comes with a well-rounded suite of fforementioned digital instrument display, which integrates all essential rider data into one convenient location. It also features LED lighting, which provides superior illumination compared to traditional bulbs, enhancing visibility and safety at night. While the bike may not come with as many advanced technologies found on larger touring bikes, the available ABS system and adjustable suspension are useful inclusions that contribute to the bike’s overall performance and safety. The Vulcan S is designed to be straightforward and easy to maintain, with a focus on usability and practicality.


Conclusion: The Kawasaki Vulcan S is a highly versatile cruiser that strikes a perfect balance be performance and classic cruiser aesthetics. Its powerful 649cc engine offers enough performance for both new riders and experienced riders who want a bike that is fun and practical. With its adjustable ergonomics, comfortable ride, and sleek design, the Vulcan S is well-suited for a wide range of riders, making it an excellent option for anyone looking for a reliable cruiser that stands out from the crowd. Whether you're looking for an easy-to-handle cruiser for city commutes or a comfortable ride for longer trips, the Vulcan S offers exceptional value in its class.
