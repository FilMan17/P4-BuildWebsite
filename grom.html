Full Description

The Honda Grom is a versatile and highly popular compact motorcycle that offers a perfect blend of performance, fun, and accessibility. Powered by a 124.9cc air-cooled, single-cylinder engine, the Grom is engineered for both efficiency and performance, making it ideal for urban commuting, recreational riding, and as a beginner’s entry point into the world of motorcycling. Despite its small engine size, the Grom delivers a surprising amount of power for its weight, with a top speed of around 55–60 mph and a lively, engaging ride that appeals to both new riders and seasoned enthusiasts looking for a fun, low-maintenance motorcycle. Its 9.7 horsepower (depending on the market) makes it more than capable of handling daily commutes or short weekend rides with ease, all while offering excellent fuel efficiency, which means fewer stops for gas and more time spent riding.


Engine and Performance: The Honda Grom’s 124.9cc engine is a simple, air-cooled, single-cylinder unit that offers dependable performance with an emphasis on ease of use and low maintenance. The engine delivers its power in a smooth, linear fashion, which helps new riders build confidence while allowing more experienced riders to enjoy its agility and responsiveness. The Grom is paired with a 4-speed manual transmission, providing an engaging experience that teaches essential motorcycling skills like gear shifting and clutch control. While it doesn’t produce a high amount of horsepower, the Grom’s engine excels in terms of practicality and fun, offering enough acceleration for tight city streets and twisty backroads.


Chassis and Handling: One of the most notable aspects of the Honda Grom is its ultra-lightweight and compact chassis. Weighing just around 229 pounds (104 kg), the Grom is easy to maneuver, making it ideal for riders of all experience levels. The frame is built with steel and designed to provide strength while keeping weight low, which contributes to the bike’s excellent handling and agility. The 12-inch wheels, which are smaller than those found on larger motorcycles, allow the Grom to handle tight corners and congested city streets with ease. The front suspension consists of a 31mm telescopic fork, while the rear is equipped with twin shock absorbers, both offering a comfortable yet responsive ride. While the suspension isn’t designed for extreme off-roading or aggressive sport riding, it does an excellent job of handling everyday street conditions and delivering a smooth ride for a variety of riders.


Braking and Safety: The Honda Grom is equipped with a solid braking system that provides ample stopping power for its size and performance capabilities. The front brake is a 220mm disc brake, paired with a single-piston caliper, offering solid stopping force that is responsive and reliable. The rear brake is a 190mm disc brake, which complements the front braking system for balanced, controlled stopping. Combined with the bike's light weight and low center of gravity, the brakes provide excellent stability and control, even during rapid deceleration or emergency stops. Additionally, the Honda Grom features Honda’s Combined Braking System (CBS), which links the front and rear brakes together, providing a smoother and more stable braking experience, especially for less experienced riders who may not be accustomed to using both brakes independently.


Design and Features: The Honda Grom’s design combines modern styling with a playful, compact appearance that mirrors the aesthetics of larger sportbikes. With sharp lines, a muscular fuel tank, and aggressive LED lighting, the Grom looks much larger than it is, which adds to its sporty, confident presence on the road. The instrument cluster is fully digital, featuring a clear and easy-to-read LCD display that shows speed, fuel level, trip meters, time, and other important riding data. The fuel tank is relatively small, holding 1.45 gallons (5.5 liters) of fuel, but this capacity is sufficient for the Grom’s small engine and efficient performance, allowing riders to travel approximately 100 miles between fill-ups. Additionally, the Grom’s minimalist bodywork and slim design make it easy to handle and customize, appealing to riders who enjoy personalizing their motorcycles. The Grom’s customization potential is one of its biggest draws, as it’s one of the most modified bikes in its class. Owners can swap out parts for everything from different handlebars to custom exhausts, mirrors, and seat options, creating a unique bike that reflects their style. The bike's small size and extensive aftermarket support mean that you can modify it for better performance, aesthetics, or comfort, depending on your preferences.


Ergonomics and Comfort: Despite its compact size, the Grom is designed for rider comfort, especially in urban settings. The seat height of 30 inches (762mm) makes it accessible for riders of all sizes, even those with shorter inseams. The riding position is upright and relaxed, with wide handlebars that help maintain a comfortable posture while riding. The footpeg placement is also well-suited for the bike's size, allowing for a natural and relaxed riding stance. While the seat itself is small and simple, it provides adequate comfort for short-to-medium-distance rides. The bike’s ergonomic design ensures that even less experienced riders can enjoy extended rides without feeling fatigued.


Technology and Features: The 2024 Honda Grom includes several features aimed at enhancing both the riding experience and convenience. The digital instrument panel provides all the essential information in a clear, compact display, including a gear position indicator, a tachometer, and a fuel gauge. The addition of a 12V power outlet allows riders to charge devices or power accessories, which is especially useful for longer rides or for riders who want to install additional lighting or GPS units. The Grom also features a 4-speed manual transmission, which offers an engaging experience for beginners and more experienced riders alike. This transmission allows riders to learn the fundamentals of clutch control and gear shifting in a simple, manageable manner.


Conclusion: The Honda Grom is an immensely popular and practical motorcycle that offers a perfect entry point for new riders while providing plenty of fun and excitement for more experienced motorcyclists. Its lightweight frame, easy-to-manage power output, and engaging handling make it an excellent choice for city commuters or those looking for a small bike that’s easy to handle but still a blast to ride. The Grom also offers extensive customization options, making it a highly personalized bike for riders who enjoy tweaking and modifying their rides. With its reliable engine, low-maintenance design, and playful, approachable nature, the Honda Grom has solidified its place as one of the most enjoyable and versatile motorcycles in the market today.
